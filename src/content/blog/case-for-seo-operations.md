There's a version of SEO that treats every engagement as a series of audits and recommendations. The consultant delivers a list of issues. The team implements some of them. Organic performance improves modestly. The cycle repeats.

This model produces results, but they're bounded. The ceiling is set by the organisation's capacity to act on recommendations — and that capacity is rarely the constraint that gets addressed.

## What SEO operations actually means

SEO operations is the discipline of building the internal systems, processes, and cross-functional relationships that enable SEO to function as a reliable growth channel rather than an occasional project.

It encompasses:

- How SEO priorities are identified, sized, and communicated to stakeholders
- How SEO recommendations move through engineering and editorial workflows
- How performance is measured and reported in ways that drive decisions
- How knowledge is captured and distributed across the organisation
- How tooling is selected, integrated, and maintained

Most SEO engagements focus on the strategy. SEO operations focuses on the machinery that executes it.

## The coordination problem

SEO touches every part of a digital business: product, engineering, content, brand, PR, data. None of those functions reports to SEO. Getting meaningful work done requires influence, not authority — and influence requires clear communication, shared incentives, and consistent delivery.

Organisations that treat SEO as a purely technical or content discipline miss this. The teams that grow organically at scale have figured out how to make SEO work embedded in cross-functional processes, not alongside them.

## Building the operating model

A practical SEO operating model covers four areas:

**Prioritisation** — a framework for evaluating and ranking opportunities that accounts for impact, effort, and strategic alignment. This should be transparent and shared with stakeholders.

**Execution** — defined workflows for how different types of SEO work get scoped, briefed, built, and shipped. This varies by work type: technical fixes have different flows to content production.

**Measurement** — a reporting structure that connects SEO activity to business outcomes, not just traffic metrics. Revenue, pipeline, and retention are the metrics leadership cares about.

**Learning** — a process for capturing what worked, what didn't, and why — and routing those learnings back into future prioritisation.

---

The organisations that treat SEO as a function rather than a project are the ones that compound. They build the operational infrastructure once and benefit from it indefinitely.
